4.1 Canopy relief ratio (\(CRR\))

A measure of the relative shape of the canopy from lidar or altimetry. Also known as the elevation relief ratio (\(ERR\)).

\[CRR = \frac{h_{mean} - h_{min}}{h_{max} - h_{max}}\]

See also:
Parker and Russ, 2004

4.2 Mean outer canopy height {\(MOCH\)}

\(MOCH\) is the mean or average height of the outer canopy (\(Z_m\)) of a forest based on some resolution, \(X\) or \(X,Y\). For example, for aerial lidar scanning or ALS, \(MOCH\) can be calculated from a canopy height model (CHM) as the mean value of each \(Z\) over a defined \(X,Y\) coordinate space or as the mean of all values in that CHM. For terrestrial lidar (TLS), \(MOCH\) is often defined as the average of the highest lidar return (also defined as \(Z_max\)) for each \(X\) or \(X,Y\) coordinate space with a standard resolution of 1 m.

Maximum canopy height (\(H_{max}\))

4.3 Mean height (\(H\))

Mean height, also mean canopy height or \(H\) is a lidar derived metric measuring the mean return height of all lidar pulses in some \(X,Y\) defined coordinate space, and defined spatial resolution. For TLS and UAV based sensors this resolution is often 1 m, while for ALS is typically 10 to 30 m.
